This is a follow-up post to this post, wherein I started a quest for a new ViewDeck maintainer. I say quest, but I actually meant "let's just through it out there and see how it fares".

So I had already left for my job at Apple and had my first day in the US done, when I received a DM from Michael Ochs, asking if I had already found a new maintainer. At that point, I was a bit afraid I might not find one. That was fine it itself, but I really wanted to "disconnect" myself from it, even though I hadn't really touched it in a few years.

But here was Michael, volunteering for the job. While I haven't met him in person we did talk a few times over the interwebs, and he's felt like a perfect fit for the job. And so I added him to the ViewDeck organisation so he could start playing his role.

As far as I could follow, he's been doing an admirably job. Responding to PRs, categorizing, tagging and replying to issues, cleaning things up, moving things forward. Exactly what ViewDeck needed: some fresh blood to get things going again.

And I'm very happy with that.

Today, I have removed myself as an admin and member from the ViewDeck organisation. I have played my role in this project and now it's time for somebody else to do that. My commits are there forever and I keep a private copy (right until when Michael took over) for myself. And besides, my work at Apple doesn't mix with open source work, so it's for the best.

So thank you, Michael, and have fun with it. ðŸ˜‰
